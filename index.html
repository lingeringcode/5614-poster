Course Description (draft, subject to change)

This course introduces multiple frameworks and methodologies available to study the rhetorics of visualizing data and other kinds of information. We will examine how the boundaries that data and information set impact its visualization, as well as impact its audiences differently across intersections of gender, race, and class. To accomplish this aim, we will first define the field more broadly by surveying its exigencies and definitions. Then, we will draw connections and boundaries between visual rhetoric and areas such as technical communication, critical data studies, disability studies, feminism, and critical race theory. Throughout the course we will draw from contemporary and historical cases and learn a sample of fundamental data-design practices.
